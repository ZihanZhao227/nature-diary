Product

Problem & value proposition

Many outdoor enthusiasts love photographing landscapes and wildlife but hate constantly pulling out a phone or carrying a heavy camera, so they miss spontaneous moments or feel pulled out of the experience. Nature Diary turns your glasses into a quiet “capture layer” and your phone into a personal nature encyclopedia.

What Nature Diary does

  • Glasses capture scenes when you press a physical button or say a simple voice cue.
  • Images are sent to the phone app and cloud backend for identification.
  • Computer vision models return species names, taxonomy, distribution, risk to humans, and more.
  • Each capture becomes a card in your private collection, with optional voice notes and emotions.

Why it is different

  • Privacy-by-design: capture is always opt-in; faces and bystanders can be blurred by default.
  • Outdoor-first hardware: sunglasses-like form factor, tuned camera, rugged and easy to clean.
  • Collector mindset: focus on quietly “collecting” nature, not posting to social media.

Architecture

Glasses → App → Cloud: how it fits together

1. Glasses

  • Physical capture button + wake word voice trigger.
  • Camera tuned to human-eye perspective for better memory recall.
  • On-device pre-processing: basic encryption and privacy filters.

2. Mobile app

  • Receives captures, uploads to cloud, shows status.
  • Displays species cards, maps, and timeline of trips.
  • Stores optional voice notes and emotions as “memory snapshots”.

3. Cloud & models

  • Fine-tuned open-source vision models (e.g., ViT-style) for species identification.
  • Privacy workflows: opt-in capture, face blurring, sensitive-content flags.
  • Future: community datasets and “Nature Quest” challenge logic.
Market & users

Who this is for

Target segment

  • Serious hikers, backpackers, and outdoor travelers who already spend on premium gear (Arc’teryx, Patagonia, etc.).
  • Birders and nature “collectors” who enjoy logging species and trips.
  • Early adopters in university outdoor clubs, youth programs, and summer camps.

  • Birdwatching and nature travel account for tens of billions of dollars in spend.
  • Most photos are now captured on phones, but many users dislike phones on the trail.
Business & events

From smart glasses to “Nature Quest” festivals

Business model

  • Hardware: Nature Diary smart glasses (approx. USD 200–400) as the primary entry point.
  • Freemium app: free basic identification and limited storage; Pro tier with unlimited storage and richer data.
  • Events: curated “Nature Quest” outdoor challenges where glasses are required to play, generating ticket, sponsorship, and streaming-side revenue.

Signature “Nature Quest” idea

  • Seasonal scavenger-hunt style events in parks: teams race to find and capture specific species and landscapes.
  • Core users qualify through in-app collections; volunteers earn free access by helping run the event.
  • Designed to turn Nature Diary from a tool into a cultural moment for outdoor explorers.
